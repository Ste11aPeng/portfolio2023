Pulp Viability

Context:

This artwork, three vases crafted from pulp, emphasizing sustainability and the cycle of life. To me, a vase is not merely a container but a bridge connecting the bustling urban existence with the tranquility of nature. My childhood fascination with wildflowers and the practice of using fallen leaves as bookmarks have deepened my connection to nature‚Äôs simple joys.

In this piece, vases and flowers symbolize the rich tapestry of human emotions, mirroring the cycles of life and the inherent beauty in growth and decay. The choice of a pulp vase reinforces the message of sustainability, highlighting the potential of repurposed materials to inspire reflection on our environmental impact.

Research

We can see that the composition of paper is relatively simple, and a large part of it is just plant fiber.

At this time, I wondered if I could use paper with different fiber proportions to explore different paper materials if this method could achieve the best choice for the production of other products.
Also, what if I used the raw materials directly in the works to express our thoughts on the composition of the vase?

Ideation

My inspiration for this work comes from a deep-rooted love for nature that has flourished since my childhood. To many, a vase may seem like a mundane object, easily overlooked and deemed non-essential in the grand scheme of home decor. Yet, from a young age, I found myself drawn to the simplicity and beauty of wildflowers picked from the roadside, using fallen leaves as bookmarks, and seeing the humble vase as a bridge between bustling city life and the serene embrace of nature. It's a symbol of how nature‚Äôs tranquility can permeate our lives, even in the smallest of ways.

Material Experimentation:

During the material experimentation phase, I delved into the process of recycling by collecting various types of waste paper from trash bins. Following the depicted steps, I transformed these discarded papers into pulp. To enhance its malleability and shape retention, I incorporated white glue into the mixture. This adjustment allowed for easier deformation and stabilization of the form.

Paper Samples:

After crafting three initial prototypes, I proceeded to create a range of paper samples. This iterative process was aimed at refining the technique to achieve the best possible outcome, focusing on the interplay of texture, durability, and aesthetic appeal in the creation of my pulp vases (show as followed).
