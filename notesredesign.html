Notes Redesign

Context:

I've always relied on Notes to jot things down, whether I’m on my iPhone or Mac. But over time, I noticed a few things that just didn’t feel as smooth or efficient as they could be.

That’s what got me thinking about how the app could be improved, and it led me to dig deeper into redesigning it with a better experience in mind.

Role:

User Flows
Functional Prototypes
Competitive Benchmarking
Wireframing
Interaction Design

Timeline:

2023 Winter / 5 weeks:
2 week research;
1 week prototype & design;
2 week testing;


Project Quick Look





Why Redesign?

01 Firstly, I mapped out the current UX flow

Based on identifying specific moments where users face obstacles in their note-taking process. Through this analysis, I highlighted key areas that impede productivity.

Pain Point 1

Audio limitation

After the user launches the app, the flow pushes them toward text input, without presenting an obvious option to record audio.

Pain Point 2

Can't sketch

In the note taking, users might assume they can sketch or highlight on top of the text paragraphs, but it can not.

Pain Point 3

No timestamp

The current flow ends with saving and organizing the note, but there’s no clear indicator of when different sections of the note were created or edited.



02 Secondly, Benchmark Notes against leading competitors

The competitive analysis helps ensure that my redesign isn't just solving problems in isolation but is also positioning Notes competitively in the broader market.



03 Thirdly, Define Goals and Metrics for Success

Working closely with two other designers and one developer, we collectively identified and prioritized the most critical goals and business metrics for the redesign. Through team discussions, we ensured that our choices aligned with both user needs and business objectives. Together, we focused on how to measure the efficiency improvements, user engagement, and technical scalability of the product.





Data Synthesis

After completing the "Why Redesign" phase, where I identified core pain points and inefficiencies in the current app, it became essential to deeply understand user behavior and expectations. So for the Data Synthesis phase, I selected these 3 methodologies that ensure our design strategy were grounded in user behavior and needs.

Methodologies 1

Persona

helped to empathize with key user groups, ensuring we designed with specific needs in mind.

Methodologies 2

User Journey

allowed us to visualize how users interact with Notes over time, mapping out critical touchpoints where we could improve workflow and reduce friction.

Methodologies 3

Card Sorting

was chosen to refine how users naturally categorize features and actions. This ensures that the interface and note organization align with user expectations..







Introducing New Notes




Voice-to-Text / Dictation

Effortless Dictation

Now you can easily convert recordings into text. Capture your thoughts on the go and refine them later with just a tap.

Pain Killer for Audio limitation





Enhanced Sketch

Smart Sketching

Adding a new sketch is now smarter. Areas where you can't draw turn gray, while hints show where you can. This makes sketching more intuitive and your notes more organized.

Pain Killer for sketch confuse.




Time stamps

Context at a Glance

Swipe right on any note to reveal time stamps. Keep track of when your notes were created or updated, giving you better context and organization.

Pain Killer for no time record



User Testing

Goals

We aim to:

1. See if the voice-to-text/dictation feature accurately transcribes speech in different environments.
2.Assess if Smart Sketching enhances the drawing experience and note organization.

Guidance

During the testing:

1. Users tested voice-to-text in various settings with different accents, then reviewed and corrected the transcriptions.
2.Users created sketches with and without Smart Sketching to compare usability.
3.Try to find the when the notes were take.
